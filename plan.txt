1. People – Ending Poverty and Ensuring Dignity

These goals focus on basic human needs, equality, and well-being.

    SDG 1: No Poverty – End poverty in all its forms everywhere.

    SDG 2: Zero Hunger – End hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture.

    SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being – Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.

    SDG 4: Quality Education – Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.

    SDG 5: Gender Equality – Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.

2. Planet – Protecting the Earth’s Ecosystems

These goals focus on environmental sustainability and ecosystem protection.

    SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation – Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.

    SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production – Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns.

    SDG 13: Climate Action – Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.

    SDG 14: Life Below Water – Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources.

    SDG 15: Life on Land – Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, manage forests, combat desertification, and halt biodiversity loss.

3. Prosperity – Inclusive Growth and Innovation

These goals promote economic development, innovation, and infrastructure.

    SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy – Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all.

    SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth – Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work.

    SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure – Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation.

    SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities – Reduce inequality within and among countries.

4. Peace – Justice and Strong Institutions

These goals emphasize peaceful societies, justice, and strong institutions.

    SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions – Promote peaceful and inclusive societies, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable institutions.

5. Partnership – Global Solidarity

This goal emphasizes international cooperation and collective action.

    SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals – Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development.











1. SDG 1: No Poverty

    Design Concept: A humble, simple structure, perhaps a house or community center with a thatched roof. The building could use worn-out materials like recycled wood and straw to symbolize overcoming poverty.

    Elements:

        Mural depicting the transition from poverty to prosperity.

        An interactive “poverty line” visual (static) to show disparities.

2. SDG 2: Zero Hunger

    Design Concept: A greenhouse-like structure, with crops growing inside to represent food security. It could feature glass walls and roof with edible plants.

    Elements:

        Agricultural displays or small farming models.

        Interactive map showing hunger statistics (static for visual display).

        A large tree or plant growing at the center.

3. SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being

    Design Concept: A clean, medical-looking building with elements like a first aid cross or symbolic heart-shaped design.

    Elements:

        Health equipment, such as medical symbols or clean, calming white walls.

        Static infographic showing health statistics.

        Relaxation space within the building to signify mental health.

4. SDG 4: Quality Education

    Design Concept: A library or school building, with bookshelves or digital learning elements, designed to promote knowledge and learning.

    Elements:

        Books, chalkboards, and desks with educational symbols.

        Interactive displays (static) showing global literacy rates and educational resources.

        Iconic globe or a graduation cap symbolizing success.

5. SDG 5: Gender Equality

    Design Concept: A modern, sleek building with an equal symbol at its core, showcasing men, women, and non-binary people together in harmony.

    Elements:

        Large gender equality symbol (equal sign) or a balance scale.

        Murals showing women and men collaborating in various professions.

        Artifacts or symbols of empowerment and rights.

6. SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation

    Design Concept: A water-themed enclosure with clear water flowing through or around it, perhaps with fountains or small water features.

    Elements:

        Transparent walls or structures to symbolize clean water.

        Static water cycle infographic.

        Water conservation techniques illustrated.

7. SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy

    Design Concept: A futuristic energy hub with solar panels, wind turbines, and a green energy design.

    Elements:

        Solar panels on the roof, wind turbines placed nearby.

        Light installations to represent renewable energy.

        Infographic of global energy consumption.

8. SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth

    Design Concept: A bustling factory or office building, with digital screens showing economic data and growth.

    Elements:

        A bustling workspace design—desk chairs, computers, and machines to represent modern work.

        Visual charts of economic growth.

        Statues or images of people working in various industries.

9. SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

    Design Concept: A futuristic industrial building, made of metal and glass, possibly resembling a tech startup hub or a factory.

    Elements:

        Displays of new technology (robots, 3D printers, etc.).

        Static models of infrastructure (bridges, buildings).

10. SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

    Design Concept: A bridge-like structure connecting two different sections, symbolizing the bridging of gaps between inequality.

    Elements:

        Statues representing diverse groups of people.

11. SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

12. SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production

13. SDG 13: Climate Action

    Design Concept: A dynamic, circular structure representing the Earth, with climate data displayed on the walls.

    Elements:

        Earth sculptures or a globe in the center.

        Solar panels and wind turbines as part of the enclosure.

14. SDG 14: Life Below Water

    Design Concept: A transparent underwater enclosure that showcases marine life with coral reefs and fish swimming inside.

    Elements:

        Coral reefs, ocean animals, or fish within clear walls.

16. SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions

17. SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals

    Elements:

        Interconnected rings or nodes representing partnerships.
